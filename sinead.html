About Brian

I grew up in Chico, California and attended UC Berkeley for undergrad. After completing my studies at UC Berkeley, I moved to Washington, DC to work at the National Institutes of Health (NIH) for two years, before starting my graduate work at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (MSKCC) in New York. I am currently a full time graduate student, in the process of earning my Ph.D in the field of immuno-oncology. My work focuses on developing next generation therapies for pediatric cancers, such as neuroblastoma.

I have tutored math students since 2007, when I graduated from high school. More professionally, I started tutoring for SAT and GRE exams in 2014, after finishing my course work in graduate school.

While I now mainly focus on preparatory work for standardized tests such as the SATs or GREs, I also still offer tutoring in the subjects of algebra, geometry and trigonometry. My main strategy is to develop students' confidence in basic mathematics so that difficult-looking or intimidating questions can be broken down into simpler problems that can be solved easily, confidently and quickly.
